Dedan Kimathi

Dedan Kimathi Waciuri, was born on the 31stof October in the year 1920. Dedan Kimathi was the senior military and spiritual leader of the Mau Mau uprising, and since he was widely regarded as a revolutionary leader, he lead the armed military struggle against the British colonial regime in Kenya in the 1950's until his capture in 1956 and execution, 0n the 18th of February 1957.

Kenyan nationalists view him as the heroic figurehead of the Kenyan freedom struggle against the British colonial rule, while the british government saw him as a terrorist. Despite having fought for the nation's liberation, it was during the Mwai Kibaki regime that Kimathi and his fellow Mau Mau companions were officially recognized as heroes in the struggle for Kenyan Independence. This recognition culminated to the unveiling of a Kimathi statue in 2007.

The Kibaki government erected a 2.1 metre bronze statue titled Freedom Fighter Dedan Kimathi on a graphite plinth, in central Nairobi. The statue is at the junction of Kimathi Street and Mama Ngina Street. Kimathi, clad in military regalia, holds a rifle in the right hand and a dagger in the left, the last weapons he held in his struggle.
